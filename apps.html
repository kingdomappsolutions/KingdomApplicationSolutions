Progressive Web App Development

A progressive web app (PWA) for your church or ministry can enable you to effectively communicate with your congregation, ministry target area, or mission field. PWAs are lighter than hybrid mobile apps, are installed from a web browser rather than an app store, and allow for more instant updates without app store waiting times. PWAs are also easier to find through web searches than hybrid apps. PWAs can do much of the same things hybrid apps can do including receive push notifications and utilize some device hardware. Progressive web apps are becoming much more popular especially considering that 51% of smartphone users have downloaded zero apps from app stores each month. Some examples of organizations using PWAs include Trivago, Starbucks, Pinterest, and Uber.

Hybrid App Development

In contrast to PWAs, hybrid mobile apps also allow for your church or ministry to communicate with your congregation, ministry target area, or mission field. Hybrid mobile apps tend to be larger file sizes and require users to find the app on the respective app stores and download them to their device. Hybrid mobile apps are more popular, but get downloaded much less frequently. Any updates to hybrid apps could take days due to app store approval times. However, hybrid mobile apps are a great option if you want users to have a more native app feel with more device hardware functionality.
